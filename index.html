Primary Paper

A Structural Conjecture for the Infinitude of Twin Primes

The central thesis: PNT ⇒ (WDC ∧ BC) ⇒ TPC. Introduces the Bridging Conjecture (BC) and Witness Decomposition Conjecture (WDC), proves BC ⇒ TPC unconditionally, and proposes that the Prime Number Theorem alone forces the additive closure of the set of twin prime witnesses — bypassing the parity obstruction that limits sieve theory. Includes the Bridging Machine, the Gap Machine, scale invariance theorems, and Lean 4 machine-verified proofs of the core implication chain.

Earlier Papers

Bridging the Gap: Additive Structure in the Twin Prime Cofactors

Introduces the Bridging Conjecture and establishes its equivalence to TPC under Dubner’s Middle Number Conjecture. Derives a conditional lower bound π2(x) ≫ log x from additive structure alone.

The Mathematics of Dubner’s Ball

The 3D visualisation of the witness set and its progenitor structure. Each node is a twin prime witness; each filament connects a witness to its decomposition pair. The ball grows to infinity if and only if BC holds.

Heuristic Evidence for the Bridging Conjecture

Decomposition gap analysis, cluster structure, and probabilistic estimates for simultaneous bridging failure. Empirical and analytic evidence that BC is overwhelmingly likely to be true.
