Dialectic

Primary Papers

The Structural Conjecture for the Infinitude of Twin Primes

The conjecture: PNT ⇒ (WDC ∧ BC) ⇒ TPC. A single analytic fact — the Prime Number Theorem — forces the additive closure of the set of twin prime witnesses, bypassing the parity obstruction. The right-hand arrow BC ⇒ TPC is proved unconditionally; the left-hand arrow is the open conjecture. Includes Lean 4 machine-verified proofs of the full proved chain (zero sorry).

The Erdős Imperative: Bertrand’s Postulate as a Route to the Twin Prime Conjecture

CE ⇒ TPC via Bertrand’s Postulate, without appeal to the Prime Number Theorem. Formally verified in Lean 4 with zero sorry. The single remaining ingredient for a full proof of TPC is a proof of Channel Exhaustion.

Earlier Papers

Logical Implications of a Bridging Conjecture for the Twin Prime Conjecture

Introduces the Bridging Conjecture and establishes its equivalence to TPC under Dubner’s Middle Number Conjecture. Derives a conditional lower bound π2(x) ≫ log x from additive structure alone.

The Mathematics of Dubner’s Ball

The 3D visualisation of the witness set and its progenitor structure. Each node is a twin prime witness; each filament connects a witness to its decomposition pair. The ball grows to infinity if and only if BC holds.

Heuristic Evidence for the Bridging Conjecture

Decomposition gap analysis, cluster structure, and probabilistic estimates for simultaneous bridging failure. Empirical and analytic evidence that BC is overwhelmingly likely to be true.
